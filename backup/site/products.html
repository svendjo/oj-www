Heavy, mobile equipment leaves the cable entry particularly exposed and prone to break-down, for instance in the event of collision.
The new flush-mounted cable gland provides maximum protection against damage as all parts, with the exception of a flat collar, are well-protected behind the panel of the equipment.
Under normal circumstances the cable floats freely from the sealing ring, only occasionally touching the bell mouth and thus preventing wear as a result of friction between the cable guard and the cable.

Cord Grip Bush
Kabelverschraubungen

-The Cord Grip Bush has a unique bridge, which has been designed so it can be used upside down, allowing numerous possible cable diameters.

The Cord Grip Bush has been around for decades.

To offer the greatest possible safety the clamp bridge can only be opened using tools. With the older version there was a risk that the clamp bridge would end up at an angle on the lead with only one screw engaged. This made the clamp bridge worthless.

This new Cord Grip Bush cannot be fitted incorrectly, as the clamp bridge can only be moved in the direction of the screw axis. This garantees that both screws will engage, ensuring optimum safety.
The ends of the new clamp bridge are wider than the middle section, which secures that both screws reach equally far into the threads. With two skids in the cord grip bush, the position of the clamp bridge is always secured.
